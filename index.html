Harlem Is Changing - Big Time! But, is this the Second Renaissance?

If you've never been to Harlem or haven't visited in a while, now is the time to head Uptown. It's in the midst of what some call its "second renaissance." However, it is a renaissance unlike the first.

Today, the area is experiencing a rebirth, so to speak, but one not in the artistic and literary sense of the 1920s and early 30s. It is occurring because of the expanding Manhattan real-estate boom north of 96th Street.

With the interest in Harlem's real-estate, has also come a renewed interest in the area's history and culture. Tourism is big business, bringing in people from as far away as Europe and Asia. Any day of the week you are bound to see tour buses creeping through long-neglected neighborhoods. But the best way to see Harlem is on foot, traveling from north to south.
